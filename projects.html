Sauron Algorithm

I started working on this project when I wanted to combine my interests of stock trading and coding. I was facinated with the idea that computers and algorithms could be used to find patterns within the stock market and was convinced that these patterns could be exploited to beat the house. The issue is, I honestly had no idea where to start. This is actually how most of my projects go, but I do think this leads to a lot of learning in areas a person isn't familar in. So, like most projects, I started by researching into the topic and how I could make an idea into a reality. The issue is, with quantitative finance, there are very limited resources online compared to my previous projects. This led me to the r/quant and r/algotrading subredddits where I was trying to understand wth was going on. Being at best, moderaty helpful, I decided I needed more specific resources so I purchased The Man Who Solved the Market, which is a biography on the life of Jim Simmons, Who was the founder of Ren tech, arguably the first quant firm. I thoroughly enjoyed it but it was surreal as Simons passed while I was reading it. However, the book didn't really offer any specifics when it comes to creating new trategies or finding new singals. More of an general view. So one day when browsing reddit I saw a post from a user on a mean reversion strategy. This excited me as it was the first time I had actually saw a specific strategy and it's results. So I decided to try to replicate this strategy and mess around with it to figure out how/why it works. when doing so I accidentaly back tested a different mean reversion strategy. Surprisingly, this new strategy resulted in better annual returns but a lower sharpe. I was very happy with the innital results as it averaged 22.3% average annual returns for the past 40 years. I eventually got this strategy to peak at roughly 48.11% annual returns in the past decade in back testing after testing the strategy on 24 different securiteis then further adjusting the conditions. I know, severly overfitted, however, I was eager to implement the strategy so I created a new file that gathers current data on a security, runs it through the algo, then talks to a broker to either buy or sell. it will then email me the daily results along with some other metrics. This process I entirely autmoated to run at a given time on a daily basis. I know it would be wiser to paper trade for a year, however, I am eager to test out the strategy with real money, along with the fact that there is very little risk in the strategy. On June 1st 2024 I innitaed the strategy with $1000 and it's been trading live for the past 2 weeks, so far up 2.53%. However, I'm now very bored without a project as this is kind of working too well, so I have now started planning my next project which I want to be very significant, being a lot more impressive of a strategy.

UPDATE: Exactly 1 month into running this strategy live and the portfolio has risen 4.05%
